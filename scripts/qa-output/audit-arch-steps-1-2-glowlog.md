## Check 1: Grounding Rule (Step 1 — AI Expectations)

**Requirement**: Step 1 should use hedging language like "user-reported" or "according to reviews" when making competitor claims, NOT stating competitor weaknesses as absolute facts.

**Result: PASS**

Step 1 consistently uses hedging and attribution language throughout. Examples:

- Line 5-6: "Across competitors like Skincare Routine: BasicBeauty and Skin Bliss, **users report** losing all product data after app restarts"
- Line 10: "**According to user reviews**, Skan locks everything behind a subscription from launch, Skin Bliss blocks 99% of features, and Charm uses misleading free trials"
- Line 16: "The most detailed **negative review** across all 10 competitors (FeelinMySkin, 2 stars) describes a user spending hours..."
- Line 20-21: "Skincare Routine app **received multiple 1-star reviews** specifically for being The Ordinary-only"
- Line 35: "**According to user reviews**, Charm charged users without consent"
- Line 42: "Skan's **alleged data practices** (per Reddit reviews)"
- Line 50-51: "Routinely **crashes during its core photo analysis feature**... **according to user reviews**"
- Line 82: "users **report** data selling allegations and privacy lawsuits in reviews"
- Line 166: "the privacy anxiety generated by Skan's **alleged** data practices **according to user reviews**"

The output properly attributes competitor claims to user reviews and avoids stating unverified claims as absolute facts. The grounding rule is well-observed.
